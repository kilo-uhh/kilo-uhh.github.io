Our approach

A healthy ocean rests on healthy people.

Western marine science often reads that backwards. We turn it right side up. We are informed by many tools and traditions, but we hold ourselves as stewards and bearers of culture, accountable first to the places and communities our data describes.

Why accessible tools

A day at sea can cost thousands. A PlanktoScope costs about $500.

Lowering the cost of observation widens who gets to ask questions about the ocean, and who gets to answer them.

What we study

Questions that drive our research

Can a satellite read the health of a reef?

We use hyperspectral and multispectral data to retrieve water quality, light, and shallow bathymetry from orbit, then test those readings against independent measurements.

EMITPACE Sentinel-2ICESat-2

What can one drop of seawater tell us?

We build and deploy open-hardware instruments so observation can happen anywhere, by anyone. Low-cost imaging and sensing turn a single sample into data the community can own.

PlanktoScopeSTELLA open hardware

How does the whole ahupuaʻa shape the sea?

The land and the ocean are one continuous system. We follow water from the watershed to the coast to ask how what happens mauka shapes the health of the reef and the kai.
